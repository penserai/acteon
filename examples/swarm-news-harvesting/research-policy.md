## 1. EU Council Agrees Position to Streamline AI Rules (Digital Omnibus)

**Date:** 13 March 2026
**Source:** [Consilium — Council agrees position to streamline rules on Artificial Intelligence](https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/03/13/council-agrees-position-to-streamline-rules-on-artificial-intelligence/)

The Council of the EU formally adopted its negotiating position on the Digital Omnibus on AI (part of the "Omnibus VII" simplification package). The position accepts key delays to high-risk AI system obligations: stand-alone high-risk AI systems must comply by **2 December 2027**, and high-risk AI embedded in regulated products by **2 August 2028** — pushing back the original August 2026 deadline by up to 16 months. The Council position now enters trilogue negotiations with the European Parliament and Commission, expected through autumn 2026.

## 2. European Parliament Committees Back AI Act Delay (101–9 Vote)

**Date:** 18–19 March 2026
**Report reference:** A10-0073/2026
**Sources:**
- [MEPs reach preliminary political agreement on AI omnibus — IAPP](https://iapp.org/news/a/meps-reach-preliminary-political-agreement-on-AI-omnibus)
- [EU Parliament committee backs AI Act delay with fixed 2027 deadline — PPC Land](https://ppc.land/eu-parliament-committee-backs-ai-act-delay-with-fixed-2027-deadline/)
- [The EU Voted 101–9 to Delay Its Own AI Law — Medium](https://medium.com/@marc.bara.iniesta/the-eu-voted-101-9-to-delay-its-own-ai-law-a14f0e361f8e)

The Committee on Civil Liberties, Justice and Home Affairs (LIBE) and the Committee on Internal Market and Consumer Protection (IMCO) voted **101 in favour, 9 against, 8 abstentions** to adopt a joint report on the Digital Omnibus on AI. Rapporteurs: **Arba Kokalari** (IMCO) and **Michael McNamara** (LIBE, Irish MEP). A preliminary political agreement had been reached on **11 March 2026** ahead of the formal vote.

**Key deadlines set by the committees:**
- High-risk AI systems (Annex III, standalone): **2 December 2027** (replacing original August 2026 deadline)
- High-risk AI systems (Annex I, embedded in regulated products): **2 August 2028**
- AI-generated content marking compliance: **2 November 2026**

The committees replaced the Commission's flexible, decision-triggered mechanism with **fixed statutory dates**. The rapporteurs stated: *"a postponement of the application date is necessary, considering the delayed preparation of standards."* The European Parliament also noted: *"the aim is to provide legal certainty and allow more time for technical standards, guidance and national authorities to prepare."*

**Key policy changes introduced:**
- **Deepfake/nudifier ban:** Prohibits AI systems from creating *"realistic images or videos so as to depict sexually explicit activities or the intimate parts of an identifiable natural person, without that person's consent"* (exemptions for platforms with effective safety measures). **MEP Sergey Lagodinsky** (German) framed this as addressing *"how much power we are willing to give AI to degrade people."*
- **Bias detection safeguard:** Adds *"clearer conditions for using sensitive personal data to detect and correct bias in high-risk systems, under strict safeguards."*
- **SME scope extension:** Extended SME protections to cover *"small mid-cap enterprises."*
- **AI literacy:** Adopted as a *"supportive requirement"* — a middle position between the original Act's hard obligation and the Commission's proposal of mere encouragement.
- **AI Office supervisory scope:** Refined to exclude certain product-regulated systems.

**Industry reaction:** The Information Technology Industry Council warned the agreement creates *"worrying signals,"* particularly the shortening of the generative AI transparency grace period to **three months**, creating compliance uncertainty. Forty-eight EU trade associations urged rollback of additional requirements, citing overlapping regulations for companies already covered under sectoral frameworks.

The plenary vote on Parliament's full position was scheduled for **26 March 2026** (Brussels session). Trilogue negotiations with the Council are expected through autumn 2026.

## 3. European Commission Misses AI Act Guidance Deadline

**Date:** February 2026 (statutory deadline: 2 February 2026)
**Sources:**
- [European Commission misses deadline for AI Act guidance on high-risk systems — IAPP](https://iapp.org/news/a/european-commission-misses-deadline-for-ai-act-guidance-on-high-risk-systems)
- [CDT Europe's AI Bulletin: February 2026 — CDT](https://cdt.org/insights/cdt-europes-ai-bulletin-february-2026/)

The European Commission missed its **2 February 2026** statutory deadline to publish guidance on **Article 6** of the AI Act — the provision that determines whether an AI application qualifies as "high-risk." The Commission had also not yet published the technical harmonised standards that organisations need to demonstrate compliance. European standardization bodies also missed their **2025 deadlines** and are now targeting end of 2026.

**Named officials:**
- **Renate Nikolay** (Deputy Director-General for Communications Networks, Content and Technology): *"These standards are not ready, and that's why we allowed ourselves in the AI omnibus to give us a bit more time."*
- **Laura Caroli** (former policy advisor to Parliament co-rapporteur Brando Benifei): *"It's just not there, and it is supposed to give clarity."*
- **Brando Benifei** (MEP, Parliament AI Act co-rapporteur) — referenced as Caroli's former principal.

**Status after deadline:** Commission officials indicated they planned draft guidelines by end of February 2026, with final adoption potentially in March or April 2026. High-risk compliance enforcement is currently scheduled for **August 2026**, with the Digital Omnibus proposing to push this back by up to 16 months.

**Industry reaction:** Chamber of Progress urged: *"policymakers should give companies the same breathing room they have afforded themselves."*

This failure to meet its own implementation deadlines is a primary justification for the Omnibus delays — the Commission cannot enforce rules for which it has not yet published guidance or standards.

## 4. EU Parliament Approves Council of Europe AI Convention

**Date:** 11 March 2026
**Source:** [EU Parliament backs EU conclusion of the Council of Europe Framework Convention on AI — Council of Europe](https://www.coe.int/en/web/artificial-intelligence/-/eu-parliament-backs-eu-conclusion-of-the-council-of-europe-framework-convention-on-artificial-intelligence)

The European Parliament approved the EU's accession to the **Council of Europe Framework Convention on Artificial Intelligence and Human Rights, Democracy and the Rule of Law** — an international treaty on AI governance. This positions the EU as a formal signatory to a global AI rights framework, complementing the domestic AI Act enforcement structure.

## 5. EU Parliament Calls for Copyright Clarification on Generative AI

**Date:** Early March 2026
**Source:** [Plenary round-up — March I 2026 — EP Think Tank](https://epthinktank.eu/2026/03/13/plenary-round-up-march-i-2026/)

MEPs adopted an own-initiative report from the Legal Affairs (JURI) Committee calling for **legal clarification on copyright and generative AI**. The report urges the Commission to address the legal grey zone around AI training data and AI-generated content reproduction, ahead of expected Commission guidance later in 2026.

## 6. European Parliament Built-In AI Debate

**Date:** March 2026
**Source:** [The European Parliament Should Manage Built-In AI, Not Disable It — Center for Data Innovation](https://datainnovation.org/2026/03/the-european-parliament-should-manage-built-in-ai-not-disable-it/)

Policy debate is ongoing around built-in/embedded AI systems in consumer devices and software. The Center for Data Innovation argues that Parliament's current amendments risk being overly restrictive on embedded AI (e.g., AI features built into operating systems or productivity software), and advocates for managed oversight rather than prohibition — a live tension heading into trilogue negotiations.
