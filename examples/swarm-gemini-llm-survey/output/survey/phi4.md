# Research Survey: Microsoft Phi-4

## Overview
Microsoft's **Phi-4** is the latest generation of the Phi family of Small Language Models (SLMs), released in phases starting in December 2024. Building on the success of Phi-3, the Phi-4 family pushes the "Pareto frontier" of performance-per-parameter, achieving frontier-level reasoning (math, science, coding) in models small enough to run on-device.

The core philosophy remains **"Data over Scale"**, utilizing high-quality synthetic data and curriculum-based training to outperform models many times their size.

## Architecture Improvements
Phi-4 introduces several key technical upgrades over the Phi-3 series:

1.  **Tokenizer Update:** Shifted to a `tiktoken`-based tokenizer (similar to GPT-4o) with a vocabulary of **100,352** (Base) or **200,064** (Mini). This significantly improves multilingual efficiency and performance compared to the previous Phi-3 tokenizer.
2.  **Extended Context:** Models support a **128K token context window** natively (Phi-4 Base initially launched with 16K but was extended).
3.  **Data-Centric Training:** Trained on **~10 trillion tokens** (Base) and **~5 trillion tokens** (Mini). The training mix heavily features synthetic data generated by GPT-4o, curated web content, and academic textbooks.
4.  **Reasoning Techniques:** 
    *   **Reasoning-Plus** variants use **GRPO (Group Relative Policy Optimization)**, allowing the model to "think" through problems by generating internal reasoning chains (COT) before answering.
    *   **Multimodal Mid-Fusion:** The 5.6B model uses a **SigLIP-2** vision encoder, projecting visual tokens directly into the Phi-4 backbone's embedding space.

## Performance & Benchmarks
Phi-4 models excel in benchmarks that measure logic, math, and science, often rivaling GPT-4o-mini and Llama-3 70B.

### Small Model Performance (Phi-4-mini 3.8B vs. Phi-3.5-mini)
| Benchmark | Phi-4-mini (3.8B) | Phi-3.5-mini (3.8B) | Note |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :--- |
| **MATH** | **64.0%** | 48.5% | Massive reasoning jump. |
| **GSM8k** | **88.6%** | 86.2% | State-of-the-art for <4B. |
| **MMLU** | 67.3% | **69.0%** | Slight drop in general trivia. |
| **GPQA** | 25.2% | **30.4%** | Trade-off for reasoning focus. |

### Reasoning Performance (Phi-4 Reasoning Plus 14B)
*   **AIME 2025:** 82.5% (Competitive with o1-mini and DeepSeek-R1).
*   **GPQA:** 56.1% (Outperforms Llama-3 70B).

## Actionable Insights for the Team
1.  **Reasoning vs. Knowledge:** Choose Phi-4 if the task requires complex math, coding, or logical tool-use. If the task is purely about world knowledge/trivia, Phi-3.5-mini or a larger Llama model might be more robust.
2.  **On-Device Deployment:** Phi-4-mini is the current state-of-the-art for 3.8B models in math/coding. It is highly recommended for mobile or offline applications using **ONNX Runtime**.
3.  **Multimodal Potential:** The 5.6B model is a strong candidate for UI/GUI grounding and document understanding tasks, offering better performance than most 7B-13B multimodal models.
4.  **Function Calling:** Unlike earlier Phi versions, Phi-4 natively supports function calling, making it suitable for agentic workflows.
