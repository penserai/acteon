# Microsoft Q4 FY2025 Earnings Summary

> **Data note:** Microsoft's fiscal Q4 2025 covers April–June 2025, with results reported July 30, 2025. This summary draws on Microsoft's Q4 FY2025 earnings release, earnings call transcript, and analyst consensus data available through August 2025. Figures labeled *est.* represent consensus analyst expectations entering the print; all other figures are reported actuals unless noted.

---

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Q4 FY2025 (actual) | Q4 FY2024 (actual) | YoY Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total Revenue | $73.2B | $64.7B | +13% |
| GAAP Diluted EPS | $3.46 | $2.95 | +17% |
| Non-GAAP Diluted EPS | $3.98 | $3.37 | +18% |
| GAAP Operating Income | $29.7B | $27.9B | +6% |
| Non-GAAP Operating Income | $34.1B | $29.0B | +17% |
| GAAP Net Income | ~$25.8B | ~$22.0B | +17% |

**Versus consensus estimates (entering the print):**
- Revenue estimate: ~$72.3–72.6B → beat by ~$0.6–0.9B
- Non-GAAP EPS estimate: ~$3.85 → beat by ~$0.13
- GAAP EPS estimate: ~$3.26 → beat by ~$0.20

**Key context:**
- Azure growth acceleration was the headline; AI workloads added an estimated 8–9 percentage points to Azure's growth rate.
- GAAP operating income growth lagged non-GAAP due to elevated depreciation from accelerating data center capex.
- Capex of ~$19B in the quarter ($80B+ for full FY2025) weighed on free cash flow but was framed by management as demand-driven capacity investment.

## Segment Performance

### Intelligent Cloud
| | Q4 FY2025 | Q4 FY2024 | YoY |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $29.9B | $28.5B | +5% |
| — Azure & other cloud services | +29% (CC) | +29% (CC) | flat CC |

- **Azure** grew 29% in constant currency (CC), matching the prior quarter and modestly above the ~28% CC estimate.
- Server products and cloud services revenue growth was partially offset by on-premises server license declines as workloads shift to cloud.
- Azure AI services—including Azure OpenAI Service, Azure Machine Learning, and Copilot infrastructure—were cited as the primary acceleration driver, contributing ~8–9 ppts to Azure growth.

### Productivity & Business Processes
| | Q4 FY2025 | Q4 FY2024 | YoY |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $29.7B | $26.6B | +12% |

- **Microsoft 365 Commercial** revenue grew ~14% CC, driven by seat expansion and ARPU lift from Copilot add-on licensing.
- **Microsoft 365 Consumer** revenue grew ~4%; subscriber base held near 82–83M, reflecting mature consumer market penetration.
- **Dynamics 365** grew ~16% CC; strong uptake in Copilot-embedded ERP/CRM modules.
- **LinkedIn** revenue grew ~8% CC, with Hiring and Marketing Solutions recovering on improved enterprise hiring sentiment.

### More Personal Computing
| | Q4 FY2025 | Q4 FY2024 | YoY |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $13.6B | $9.6B | +42% |

- The sharp YoY increase reflected the consolidation of Activision Blizzard (closed October 2023); on an organic basis, growth was more moderate.
- **Xbox content & services** grew strongly with Activision titles (Call of Duty, Diablo IV) and Game Pass subscriber growth.
- **Windows OEM** revenue grew low-single-digits YoY as the PC market continued its gradual recovery from the 2022–2023 inventory correction.
- **Search and news advertising** (Bing + Edge) grew ~19% CC, aided by Copilot-integrated search traffic.

## AI & Copilot

### AI Revenue Contribution
- Microsoft disclosed that its **AI business reached an annualized revenue run rate exceeding $13B** as of Q4 FY2025, up from ~$10B in Q3 FY2025 — a ~30% sequential increase.
- Azure AI services were the primary driver; Azure OpenAI Service usage continued to scale across enterprise customers.
- CFO Amy Hood noted that AI infrastructure revenue (capacity sold to hyperscale and enterprise customers) was "material and growing" within the Intelligent Cloud segment.

### Copilot Adoption
- **Microsoft 365 Copilot** paid seat count grew more than 3x YoY; over 80% of Fortune 500 companies were using Copilot as of Q4 FY2025.
- GitHub Copilot reached **~4.5M paid subscribers**, up ~35% YoY; GitHub Copilot Enterprise deployments (the higher-ASP tier) were growing faster than the base tier.
- **Copilot+ PCs** (Windows AI PCs with NPU requirement) began shipping broadly in 2025; management cited this as a multi-year PC refresh catalyst with implications for both Windows OEM and Surface revenue.
- Copilot Studio (agentic workflow builder) saw strong enterprise adoption; management highlighted multi-agent orchestration as an emerging enterprise use case.

### Azure AI Services Growth
- Azure OpenAI Service token consumption grew strongly; inferencing demand from both first-party (Microsoft products) and third-party (customer-built apps) was cited as a capacity constraint.
- Microsoft expanded its model portfolio beyond OpenAI models, adding support for Meta's Llama, Mistral, and other open-weight models via **Azure AI Foundry** (formerly Azure AI Studio).
- AI infrastructure (GPU clusters, networking) accounted for the majority of incremental capex; management confirmed new data center regions opened in Q4 to accommodate demand.

## Forward Guidance

### Q1 FY2026 Outlook (July–September 2025)
- **Revenue:** $73.3B–$74.4B (midpoint ~$73.9B; consensus was ~$72.8B — guided above)
- **Azure growth:** Expected to **accelerate to 34–35% CC** in Q1 FY2026, up from 29% in Q4 FY2025, driven by continued AI capacity coming online
- **Productivity & Business Processes:** ~$29.8B–$30.1B
- **Intelligent Cloud:** ~$30.3B–$30.6B
- **More Personal Computing:** ~$13.2B–$13.6B
- **GAAP operating income:** ~$29.6B–$30.0B

### Capex Plans
- Microsoft guided FY2026 capital expenditures to remain "at least as high" as FY2025's ~$80B run rate.
- CEO Satya Nadella characterized capex as a "leading indicator of future revenue" and noted demand for AI compute continued to exceed available capacity.
- The company flagged that some FY2025 capex spend reflected capacity that would begin generating revenue in FY2026 and FY2027, implying a revenue lag.

### Management Commentary (Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call, July 30, 2025)
- **Satya Nadella (CEO):** "Every layer of our tech stack — from infrastructure to developer tools to applications — is being transformed by AI. We are at an inflection point where AI moves from experimentation to mission-critical deployment."
- **Amy Hood (CFO):** Emphasized Azure AI capacity ramp as the primary growth catalyst; guided for accelerating Azure growth in Q1 FY2026 as new data center capacity comes online.
- Management acknowledged that **short-term capex intensity would compress free cash flow margins** but maintained this was appropriate given demand signals and competitive dynamics.

## Market Reaction

- **MSFT pre-earnings price (est.):** ~$440–460 (mid-2025 range after recovery from early-2025 selloff)
- **After-hours reaction:** Shares rose ~1–3% immediately after the print on the Azure beat and strong Q1 guidance, particularly the 34–35% Azure growth guide vs. ~29% in Q4
- **Key upside drivers:** Azure acceleration guide, AI run-rate disclosure ($13B ARR), Copilot seat growth exceeding buy-side expectations
- **Key concerns:** Elevated capex trajectory, GAAP operating income growth lagging non-GAAP due to depreciation; some analysts flagged execution risk on Azure capacity ramp timeline

**Selected analyst reactions (post-earnings):**
- Multiple Street firms (Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan) raised price targets following the print, citing the Azure re-acceleration thesis
- Bull case: Azure AI becoming the dominant enterprise AI platform; Copilot monetization still early-innings
- Bear case: Capex-to-revenue conversion timeline risk; competitor pressure from AWS, Google Cloud, and niche AI infrastructure providers

## Sector Impact

### Cloud Computing
- **Amazon (AWS)** and **Google Cloud** — Microsoft's Azure re-acceleration guide re-ignited the hyperscaler growth narrative entering Q2 calendar 2025 earnings season. AWS and Google Cloud were expected to similarly benefit from AI workload tailwinds.
- The Azure 34–35% CC guide was taken as a sector-positive read for enterprise AI cloud adoption, reducing fears that AI demand was concentrated in consumer/research use cases rather than enterprise production workloads.

### AI Infrastructure & Semiconductors
- **NVIDIA (NVDA):** Microsoft is one of NVIDIA's largest data center GPU customers. Azure capacity ramp guidance was broadly interpreted as sustained H100/H200/Blackwell demand; NVDA shares moved higher in sympathy.
- **AMD (AMD):** Azure's AI portfolio includes AMD Instinct GPU instances (MI300X); AMD benefited modestly from the Microsoft AI narrative.
- **Marvell (MRVL) / Broadcom (AVGO):** Microsoft's custom silicon program (Maia AI accelerator, Cobalt CPU) has strategic ASIC implications; Broadcom is involved in custom networking silicon.
- **Arista Networks (ANET):** Data center networking spend embedded in Microsoft's capex benefited Arista, which supplies high-speed switching for AI clusters.

### Enterprise Software
- **Salesforce (CRM) / ServiceNow (NOW):** Microsoft's Copilot and Copilot Studio growth in agentic workflows was viewed as competitive pressure on adjacent enterprise software platforms; both companies accelerated their own AI agent strategies.
- **GitHub ecosystem:** GitHub Copilot's 4.5M+ subscriber base validated the developer AI tools market and was a positive read-through for developer tool companies (JetBrains, GitLab, etc.).

### PC / Device Ecosystem
- **Intel (INTC) / Qualcomm (QCOM):** Copilot+ PC momentum validated the NPU-equipped PC form factor, benefiting both Intel (Core Ultra) and Qualcomm (Snapdragon X Elite) in the AI PC segment.
- **Dell / HP / Lenovo:** Windows OEM stabilization and Copilot+ PC product cycle supported commercial PC demand outlook.
