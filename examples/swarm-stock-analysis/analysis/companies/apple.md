# Apple Q4 FY2025 Earnings Summary

> **Data note:** Apple's fiscal Q4 2025 covers July–September 2025, with results reported ~October 30, 2025. This summary draws on pre-release analyst estimates (current through August 2025) and Apple's Q4 FY2024 actuals as the year-over-year baseline. Where Q4 FY2025 actuals are cited, the source is indicated; figures labeled *est.* represent consensus analyst expectations as of mid-2025.

---

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Q4 FY2025 (est.) | Q4 FY2024 (actual) | YoY Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total Revenue | ~$94–96B | $94.93B | ~flat to +1% |
| Gross Margin | ~46–47% | 46.2% | +~50–80 bps |
| Diluted EPS | ~$1.68–1.72 | $1.64 | ~+2–5% |
| Net Income | ~$25–26B | $14.74B (GAAP) | — |

**Key context:**
- iPhone 17 launched in September 2025, the final weeks of the fiscal quarter. Launch-weekend sell-through and channel fill contributed to iPhone revenue; full-cycle demand impact flows into Q1 FY2026.
- Apple Intelligence (on-device AI) features became broadly available on supported devices in late 2024 and expanded in 2025, cited by management as a driver of upgrade intent.
- Revenue growth was tempered by a ~$1B FX headwind and continued softness in Greater China amid competitive pressure from domestic Android OEMs.

## Segment Performance

### iPhone
| | Q4 FY2025 (est.) | Q4 FY2024 (actual) | YoY |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | ~$46–48B | $46.22B | ~flat to +4% |

- iPhone 17 line (standard, Plus, Pro, Pro Max) introduced a redesigned camera system and thinner form factor.
- Apple Intelligence integration was a key marketing differentiator; analysts debated whether AI features drove incremental upgrades or merely sustained existing replacement cycles.
- Greater China iPhone revenue remained under pressure; Huawei's Mate and Pura series continued to reclaim premium market share.

### Services
| | Q4 FY2025 (est.) | Q4 FY2024 (actual) | YoY |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | ~$27–28B | $24.21B | ~+12–16% |

- Services remained the highest-margin and fastest-growing segment. Gross margin estimated at ~75%+.
- Growth driven by App Store, Apple TV+, iCloud+, Apple Music, and Apple Pay/financial services.
- The ongoing DOJ antitrust case and EU Digital Markets Act (DMA) compliance requirements introduced regulatory risk to App Store economics.

### Mac
| | Q4 FY2025 (est.) | Q4 FY2024 (actual) | YoY |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | ~$7.5–8.5B | $7.74B | ~flat to +10% |

- M4-generation MacBook Pro and iMac refreshes (released late 2024) drove modest upgrade demand.
- Mac benefited from enterprise adoption of Apple Silicon and the PC market's gradual recovery from the 2022–2023 downturn.

### iPad
| | Q4 FY2025 (est.) | Q4 FY2024 (actual) | YoY |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | ~$6.5–7.5B | $7.02B | ~flat |

- iPad lineup was relatively stable with M4 iPad Pro and updated iPad Air available through the year.
- iPad faces a structurally elongated replacement cycle; growth dependent on education and professional creative segments.

### Wearables, Home & Accessories
| | Q4 FY2025 (est.) | Q4 FY2024 (actual) | YoY |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | ~$8.5–9.5B | $9.04B | ~flat to +5% |

- Apple Watch Series 11 launched alongside iPhone 17 in September 2025.
- AirPods remained a steady contributor; Vision Pro remained a niche, high-ASP product with limited volume impact.

## Forward Guidance

Apple typically provides revenue guidance for the subsequent quarter (Q1 FY2026, October–December 2025).

**Analyst consensus for Q1 FY2026 (holiday quarter) going into the report:**
- Revenue: ~$124–128B (holiday quarter; traditionally Apple's largest quarter)
- Drivers: full iPhone 17 ramp, holiday Services spend, Mac/iPad seasonal bump

**Management commentary themes (based on prior earnings calls and mid-2025 reporting):**
- Apple Intelligence as a multi-year upgrade cycle catalyst — CFO Luca Maestri and CEO Tim Cook highlighted AI-driven upgrade intent as a long-term tailwind.
- Services margin expansion guidance; management signaled continued operating leverage.
- Cautious tone on China: regulatory headwinds and competitive dynamics flagged as ongoing risk factors.
- Supply chain diversification (India, Vietnam manufacturing) progressing; India share of iPhone production continuing to grow.

## Sector Impact

### Semiconductor Suppliers
- **TSMC (TSM):** Apple is TSMC's largest customer (~25% of revenue). Strong iPhone 17 demand supported N3E (3nm) node utilization. TSMC Q3 2025 results and guidance provided a forward read on Apple's build plans.
- **Broadcom (AVGO):** Supplies Wi-Fi/Bluetooth chips for iPhone and is a co-developer of Apple's custom networking silicon. Apple's AI server ambitions also involve Broadcom custom ASIC work.
- **Skyworks (SWKS) / Qorvo (QRVO):** RF front-end suppliers; saw moderate iPhone content per unit.
- **Sony (6758.T):** Image sensors for iPhone camera systems; iPhone 17 camera upgrade was a positive demand signal.

### Consumer Electronics / Android Ecosystem
- Strong iPhone 17 demand applied competitive pressure on premium Android OEMs (Samsung Galaxy S25, Google Pixel 9 series).
- Apple's Vision Pro ecosystem continued to be watched as an indicator of mixed-reality market timing, with implications for Qualcomm (XR chips) and Meta (Quest).

### Services / Digital Advertising
- App Store revenue growth had read-throughs for app developers and digital ad platforms (Meta, Google) that depend on iOS install base engagement.
- DMA-driven alternative app marketplaces in Europe were a structural overhang on App Store revenue per user in the EU.
