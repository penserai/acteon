# Ad Tech Sector — Q4 2025 Big Tech Earnings Impact

> **Quarter ended:** December 31, 2025 | **Earnings reported:** January–February 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Q4 2025 earnings confirmed that digital advertising remains structurally healthy, with the holiday quarter delivering broad-based outperformance across search, retail media, and video. Google's total advertising revenue reached $82.28B (+13.5% YoY), Amazon's advertising business hit $21.3B (+23% YoY), and both companies posted beats against consensus estimates. The headline results put to rest persistent fears that generative AI would cannibalize search monetization: Google Search grew +17% YoY to a record $63.1B, outpacing total ad revenue growth and demonstrating that AI Overviews are additive to — not substitutive of — the commercial search query ecosystem.

The defining structural theme of the cycle was the continued consolidation of advertising spend into walled gardens. Google Search, YouTube, and Amazon together accounted for the vast majority of measurable digital ad revenue growth in Q4. Google Network (open-web programmatic) declined -1.5% YoY, confirming that the secular shift away from open programmatic display continues. Retail media emerged as the quarter's standout growth vector: Amazon's 23% ad growth reflects the irreplaceable value of first-party, transaction-linked targeting data in a post-cookie, post-ATT environment where measurement and attribution are under sustained pressure.

AI is increasingly the infrastructure layer beneath advertising rather than a competitive threat to it. Performance Max, Amazon's relevance and bid models, and Meta's Advantage+ system are driving CPM recovery and return-on-ad-spend improvements that are sustaining and expanding advertiser budgets. The industry consensus entering 2026 is that AI makes existing ad platforms more effective, creating a rising-tide dynamic for dominant walled-garden operators — while simultaneously raising the technical and data barriers that make it harder for independent ad tech companies to compete.

## Search Advertising

### Google Search — Record Quarter

| Metric | Q4 2025 | YoY Change |
|--------|---------|------------|
| Google Search Revenue | $63.1B | +17% |
| Total Google Ad Revenue | $82.28B | +13.5% |
| Google Network Revenue | $7.83B | -1.5% |

Google Search's +17% growth in Q4 2025 was the strongest quarterly result in recent history and notably outpaced total advertising revenue growth of +13.5%. This spread — Search growing faster than the total — is analytically significant: it indicates that commercial intent queries, where advertisers compete for the highest CPMs, are growing faster than the broader display and network segments.

### AI Overviews: The Monetization Debate Resolved

The central concern entering Q4 was whether Google's AI Overviews (the conversational AI summaries shown above traditional search results) would reduce click-through rates on paid links, thereby compressing search ad revenue. Q4 2025 earnings effectively resolved this debate in favor of the bull case:

- Search revenue growth of +17% exceeded the +12–14% consensus estimate by a meaningful margin
- Google management commentary indicated that AI Overviews are driving query volume expansion, particularly for complex, multi-step queries that previously fell outside commercial search intent
- Long-tail commercial queries — historically difficult to monetize — are converting at higher rates when AI Overviews provide structured answers that surface product and service options

The operating logic: AI Overviews expand the top of the search funnel by making Google more useful for informational queries that eventually convert to commercial intent. Users who previously would have navigated directly to a brand site now interact more deeply with Google's search layer, generating additional ad impression opportunities.

### Microsoft/Bing Search Signals

Microsoft did not break out Bing-specific advertising revenue in its Q2 FY2026 earnings, but Search and News Advertising revenue within the More Personal Computing segment grew modestly. Microsoft's Copilot integration into Bing has not materially altered the competitive dynamics of search advertising — Google's +17% Search growth suggests Bing has not taken meaningful share in the commercial search market despite significant AI investments. The structural advantage of Google's query volume, advertiser auction depth, and Quality Score dataset remains intact.

## Video & Social Advertising

### YouTube — Election Comp Masks Underlying Health

| Metric | Q4 2025 | YoY Change |
|--------|---------|------------|
| YouTube Ads Revenue | $11.38B | +8.7% |

YouTube's +8.7% growth appeared soft relative to Search's +17% performance, but the deceleration is almost entirely explained by base-period effects. Q4 2024 benefited substantially from U.S. presidential election spending — political advertising is among the highest-CPM category in digital video, and its absence in an off-year Q4 created a meaningful headwind. Adjusting for the election comp, YouTube's underlying run-rate growth is estimated in the mid-teens, consistent with its multi-year trend.

The structural story for YouTube is compelling: it is the No. 1 streaming service by viewership in the United States (Nielsen data, full-year 2025), commanding more living-room viewing time than Netflix, Disney+, or any linear cable network. This makes YouTube the most cost-effective point of access to the U.S. connected TV audience for brand advertisers, with the targeting precision of a digital platform and the reach of broadcast.

YouTube Shorts monetization is also inflecting. Short-form video ad rates have historically been lower than long-form; Alphabet has been steadily closing this gap through improved Shorts ad formats and creator revenue sharing, which increases the supply of high-quality Shorts content and therefore the attractiveness of the format to brand advertisers.

### Connected TV & Streaming Advertising Dynamics

The CTV advertising market is in the midst of a structural transition as streaming services with subscription heritage (Netflix, Disney+, Max) build out ad-supported tiers to recover average revenue per user as subscription growth matures. This is a secular tailwind for the broader video advertising ecosystem:

- **Prime Video Ads** (launched Q1 2024, fully ramped by Q4 2025): Amazon's integration of advertising into Prime Video represents the most strategically significant CTV development of the cycle. Prime Video reaches 200M+ households globally. Crucially, Amazon can close the attribution loop from video ad exposure directly to Amazon purchase — a capability no other CTV operator possesses at scale. This combination of reach and purchase-intent data makes Prime Video inventory the most attractive in CTV for performance-oriented brand advertisers.

- **YouTube CTV**: YouTube's living-room footprint (streamed primarily via Smart TVs and connected devices) has made it a de facto broadcast replacement for younger demographics. Alphabet's commentary on CTV viewership growth was among the more bullish data points in the earnings call.

- **Competitive implications for Snap, Pinterest, TikTok**: Snap reported Q4 2025 revenue of ~$1.56B (+14% YoY), demonstrating resilience but also illustrating the challenge of competing for incremental brand budgets in a market where YouTube and Meta command disproportionate CPMs. Pinterest's performance advertising improvements are gaining traction with home, fashion, and retail verticals. TikTok remains the primary competitive uncertainty: its U.S. legal status entered 2026 unresolved, and the prospect of a forced divestiture or access restriction has created meaningful advertiser hedging behavior, with brands quietly shifting incremental budgets toward YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels as contingency.

### Meta (Contextual Signals)

Meta did not report in this cycle's primary window, but its Advantage+ AI targeting system — which uses machine learning to automate audience selection, creative optimization, and bid strategy — has been widely cited in agency channel checks as driving improved return on ad spend in the 12–18 months since its full rollout. The rebound of Meta's advertising business following the Apple ATT disruption is the most instructive case study in how walled-garden first-party data creates structural resilience. Meta's Q4 performance is expected to show continued high-teens to low-twenties growth when reported.

## Retail Media & E-commerce Ads

### Amazon Advertising — The Structural Outperformer

| Metric | Q4 2025 | YoY Change |
|--------|---------|------------|
| Amazon Advertising Revenue | $21.3B | +23% |
| Amazon Total Revenue | $213.4B | +14% |

Amazon's advertising segment grew +23% YoY in Q4 2025, making it the fastest-growing major advertising business of the quarter. At $21.3B for a single quarter, Amazon Advertising is now the third-largest digital advertising business in the world, behind only Google and Meta, and its growth rate exceeds both. The segment has an annualized run rate exceeding $85B — a scale that is increasingly relevant to the overall competitive landscape.

### The Closed-Loop Attribution Moat

Amazon's structural advantage in advertising is its ability to connect ad impression to verified purchase. This closed-loop attribution — impossible for open-web programmatic and unavailable to social platforms that do not operate physical e-commerce infrastructure — is the most defensible moat in digital advertising:

- **Sponsored Products** (search results placement): The highest-CPM, highest-ROI format for brands selling on Amazon; growing in proportion to Amazon's GMV growth
- **Sponsored Brands / Display**: Upper-funnel formats for brand awareness; growing as Amazon's audience data attractiveness to off-Amazon advertisers expands
- **Prime Video Ads**: Adding a CTV layer with purchase-data targeting (as described above); among the highest CPMs in all of CTV
- **Amazon DSP**: Demand-side platform that allows advertisers to use Amazon's first-party data to target audiences on third-party sites; growing as a share of total advertising revenue

### Retail Media Network Expansion

The broader retail media trend — retailers monetizing their digital shelf space and first-party customer data — is a Q4 2025 structural theme that extends well beyond Amazon:

- Walmart Connect, Kroger Precision Marketing, Target Roundel, and Instacart Ads are growing retail media networks that are taking budget from traditional trade promotion and performance marketing
- eMarketer projects U.S. retail media ad spend will surpass $100B by 2027, exceeding linear TV
- The category's defining characteristic is intent-at-point-of-purchase targeting — the highest commercial value signal available in digital advertising

### Implications for The Trade Desk and Criteo

**The Trade Desk (TTD)** is the dominant independent demand-side platform, with ~9% share of the programmatic market. Its model — charge a transparent technology fee on managed ad spend, maintain no owned media — is structurally differentiated from walled gardens. However, Q4 2025 earnings dynamics create mixed signals for TTD:

- *Positive*: CTV advertising growth benefits The Trade Desk, which has significant CTV inventory partnerships and has built OpenPath direct-publisher connections to reduce reliance on traditional ad exchanges
- *Negative*: Amazon's growth comes partly at the expense of open-web programmatic budgets; Google Network's -1.5% decline confirms the secular headwind to the programmatic ecosystem that TTD's non-CTV business depends on
- *Structural risk*: Google's Privacy Sandbox initiative — the post-cookie identity alternative — has been challenged and delayed but will ultimately reshape open-web targeting in ways that are more favorable to walled gardens than to independent DSPs

**Criteo** faces a more acute structural challenge. Its retargeting business is directly exposed to cookie deprecation, and its transition toward a retail media monetization platform (Criteo Commerce Max) is ongoing but not yet at sufficient scale to offset legacy revenue headwinds.

## AI in Advertising

### AI as Ad Infrastructure — Not a Disruptor, a Multiplier

The advertising industry's AI narrative entering 2025 was primarily about risk: would generative AI tools allow advertisers to bypass platforms (by generating organic content)? Would AI search overviews cannibalize click-through? Would synthetic media undermine brand safety? Q4 2025 earnings suggest the more important AI story is operational: AI is making advertising platforms measurably more effective, which is expanding budgets, not replacing them.

### Platform AI Systems

**Google Performance Max**: Google's fully automated campaign type, which uses AI to allocate budget across Search, Shopping, YouTube, Display, and Gmail automatically. PMax is now the default recommendation for most direct-response advertisers. Its AI optimization consistently outperforms human-managed campaigns on cost-per-acquisition metrics — Google's internal data indicates 13–20% CPA improvement for PMax vs. standard Shopping campaigns. This drives advertiser consolidation to Google: if the algorithm performs better when it has more budget to optimize across formats, advertisers have an incentive to consolidate spend rather than diversify.

**Amazon Relevance and Bid Models**: Amazon's advertising revenue growth of +23% significantly outpaces its GMV growth (~10–12%), implying CPM inflation and auction fill-rate improvement driven by AI bid optimization. Amazon's flywheel — more sellers create more competition at auction, higher CPMs fund more infrastructure, better targeting improves ROAS, which attracts more sellers — is compounding faster as AI makes each layer more efficient.

**Meta Advantage+**: As noted above, Advantage+'s AI-driven creative and audience optimization is demonstrating measurable ROAS improvements that have accelerated budget recovery post-ATT. The system's ability to optimize across creative variants without human intervention is compressing the creative production bottleneck that previously limited social ad scaling.

### Generative AI for Ad Creative

Generative AI creative tools are becoming embedded in all major advertising platforms:

- Google Asset Generation (within Performance Max): AI-generated images and headlines; adopted by 40%+ of PMax campaigns
- Amazon's AI-generated product imagery: Reduces creative production friction for SMB sellers on Sponsored Brands
- Meta's generative creative suite: Background generation, image expansion, text variation — rolled out to all advertisers in 2025

The net effect is CPM compression risk at the long tail (more inventory of lower-quality AI-generated creative) offset by CPM expansion at the high end (better AI-optimized targeting raises the value of high-intent inventory). Net-net, the platforms that control both the inventory and the targeting logic (Google, Amazon, Meta) benefit; ad creative agencies and production vendors face structural pricing pressure.

### Privacy-Preserving Advertising

The post-ATT, post-cookie era has catalyzed a shift toward privacy-preserving measurement infrastructure:

- **Conversions API / Server-Side Tagging**: Facebook CAPI, Google's server-side Tag Manager, and Amazon's Conversion API allow advertisers to share hashed first-party data (email, phone) server-to-server, bypassing browser-level tracking restrictions. CAPI adoption is now standard practice for mid-to-large advertisers.
- **Clean Rooms**: Google Ads Data Hub, Amazon Marketing Cloud, and The Trade Desk's Unified ID 2.0 coalition allow advertisers to join first-party datasets with platform data in privacy-compliant compute environments without raw data exchange.
- **Modeled Conversions**: Both Google and Meta now use AI to model conversion events that are not directly observable due to ATT/cookie restrictions, filling attribution gaps with probabilistic estimates.

These solutions disproportionately benefit large platforms with the first-party data estates and engineering resources to build them. The practical implication is that advertising attribution quality — the ability to measure what works — is improving fastest for Google, Amazon, and Meta, further widening the ROI gap versus open-web and smaller platform alternatives.

## Holiday Quarter Performance

### Q4 Seasonality — Broad Beat, No Demand Cracks

Q4 is seasonally the strongest advertising quarter due to holiday retail spending, and Q4 2025 outperformed elevated expectations:

| Advertiser | Q4 2025 Revenue | vs. Estimate | Holiday Seasonal Factor |
|-----------|----------------|--------------|------------------------|
| Google Search | $63.1B (+17%) | +~$2–3B beat | Holiday retail intent drives CPM expansion |
| YouTube | $11.38B (+8.7%) | In-line / slight beat | Election comp headwind masked underlying strength |
| Amazon Ads | $21.3B (+23%) | Beat | Prime holiday deals + sponsored product CPM peak |
| Google Network | $7.83B (-1.5%) | Slight miss | Programmatic display secular decline |

The beat on Search is particularly meaningful because Q4 CPMs are naturally elevated — the fact that growth accelerated beyond seasonal expectations indicates genuine incremental demand, not simply favorable seasonality. Agency channel checks entering Q4 indicated that brands were increasing digital budgets at the expense of linear TV, a trend that the results confirm.

### Advertiser Spending Patterns

Several dynamics shaped Q4 2025 advertiser behavior:

1. **Retail and CPG dominance**: Q4 is structurally dominated by retail, consumer electronics, and CPG advertisers. Amazon's ad growth of +23% is partly self-reinforcing — as more consumer purchases flow through Amazon, more brands allocate trade marketing to Amazon Sponsored Products.

2. **Political advertising absence**: The absence of U.S. federal election spending (a ~$2–3B tailwind in Q4 2024) created a headwind primarily for YouTube and connected TV formats, which captured disproportionate political video spend in 2024.

3. **AI/tech advertiser strength**: B2B technology advertisers — especially AI companies marketing to enterprise buyers — were a source of incremental demand in Search and LinkedIn. Google Search's +17% performance was supported by high-CPM tech vertical queries.

4. **Budget acceleration into digital**: eMarketer's post-Q4 survey data indicated that 61% of brand advertisers increased digital ad budgets in Q4 2025 versus Q4 2024, with the primary source being linear TV reallocation. The average brand reduced linear TV's share of total media spend by 3–5 percentage points.

## Impact on Ad Tech Ecosystem

### Walled Garden Consolidation vs. Open Web

The structural story of Q4 2025 is the widening gap between walled-garden ad performance and the open-web programmatic ecosystem:

| Segment | Q4 2025 Trend | Structural Outlook |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|
| Google Search | +17% | Secular growth; AI Overviews additive |
| Amazon Ads | +23% | Retail media flywheel; highest structural growth |
| YouTube | +8.7% (adj. for election ~mid-teens) | CTV market share gains |
| Meta (estimated) | High-teens to low-twenties | Advantage+ recovery; ATT headwind absorbed |
| Google Network | -1.5% | Secular decline; cookie fragmentation |
| Open-web programmatic | Estimated -3–5% ex-Google | Structural headwinds: cookies, ATT, supply fragmentation |

The divergence is stark. The walled gardens grow because they have first-party identity (Google Account, Amazon Purchase History, Meta social graph) that survives the deprecation of third-party cookies and device-level identifiers. The open web does not.

### Programmatic Display Trends

Google Network's -1.5% decline — the segment that houses AdSense, AdMob, and the Google Display Network — is the clearest signal of structural open-web programmatic decline. This segment represents Google's role as the dominant ad tech intermediary for publisher monetization. Its revenue decline, while modest in absolute terms, is directionally consistent with:

- Publishers migrating to direct deals and preferred private marketplaces to preserve CPMs
- Brand safety concerns reducing open auction demand
- The general shift of performance advertising budgets to Search, Shopping, and Social, where attribution is cleaner

The advertising businesses themselves were not the source of market concern. This creates a thesis where ad revenue quality is high while equity valuations temporarily reflect capital allocation anxiety rather than underlying business performance.

### Key Risks

**1. AI search disruption — delayed, not resolved**
The Q4 2025 data demonstrates that AI Overviews are currently additive to Search monetization. However, the longer-term question — whether next-generation AI agents that can complete commercial transactions directly will bypass the search-ad interface — is not answered by one quarter of data. OpenAI's direct integration with shopping and service providers (via GPT-4 with tools), Perplexity's shopping features, and Apple Intelligence's ability to complete tasks without a search engine are structurally different threats from AI Overviews. The 2026 trajectory of AI-native search alternatives is the highest-priority watch item for Google's long-term advertising moat.

**2. Cookie deprecation and identity fragmentation**
Google's Privacy Sandbox timeline for Chrome third-party cookie deprecation remains in flux. The repeated delays have created uncertainty that is arguably worse for the industry than a clean deprecation date, as advertisers and publishers cannot commit to new identity infrastructure without certainty about timing. When deprecation ultimately occurs, the structural beneficiaries will be first-party data holders (Google, Amazon, Meta); the structural losers will be open-web publishers and independent ad tech intermediaries.

**3. Regulatory and antitrust pressure**
The U.S. Department of Justice's monopoly case against Google's advertising technology stack (ad exchanges, publisher ad servers) received a ruling in November 2024 finding Google guilty of maintaining a monopoly in publisher ad servers and open-web display ad markets. Remedies — potentially including a forced divestiture of Google Ad Manager or DV360 — are being litigated through 2026. A forced divestiture would be the most significant structural change to the digital advertising industry since the introduction of programmatic trading. Timeline uncertainty is high, but directionally the regulatory environment is more hostile to open-web ad tech consolidation than at any prior point.

**4. TikTok resolution uncertainty**
TikTok's U.S. legal status remains unresolved entering 2026. An extended ban or forced divestiture would release approximately $7–10B in annual U.S. advertising budget that would redistribute primarily to YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, and Snapchat. This is a positive catalyst for Google and Meta, and a binary event for Snap. The uncertainty is also distorting current allocations: brands are hedging by maintaining TikTok spend while simultaneously increasing Shorts and Reels investment, inflating total social video budget in the near term.

**5. CPM cycle and macro sensitivity**
Digital advertising is a cyclical business despite its secular growth trend. A macro slowdown or consumer spending compression in 2026 would disproportionately affect upper-funnel brand advertising (YouTube, Display) while leaving lower-funnel performance advertising (Search, Amazon Sponsored Products) relatively more resilient. The premium CPM environment of Q4 2025 may not persist through 2026 if consumer confidence deteriorates, particularly given that the holiday quarter data reflects peak seasonal demand.

### Investment Framework

**Bull Case**
AI is making advertising more measurable, more automated, and more effective — all of which increase advertiser willingness to pay. Walled-garden consolidation is reducing structural complexity for large advertisers, who are increasingly concentrating spend in fewer, more measurable channels. Google Search's +17% growth in Q4 — despite AI disruption fears — demonstrates the resilience of intent-based advertising. Amazon's retail media closed loop is the single most valuable attribution signal in digital advertising and compounds as Prime membership and GMV grow. AI Overviews expanding query volume is a multi-year tailwind for Google's addressable market.

**Bear Case**
AI search alternatives represent a genuine long-term threat to query-based advertising that Q4 2025 data does not yet capture. Cookie deprecation and ATT headwinds disproportionately affect the mid-market of the advertising industry, which is where the most pricing pressure will occur. Open-web programmatic is in secular decline, creating headwinds for any company (including Google Network) exposed to that segment. Regulatory risk — particularly DOJ remedies against Google's ad tech stack — introduces binary event risk. CPM inflation is not guaranteed to persist if macro conditions soften.
