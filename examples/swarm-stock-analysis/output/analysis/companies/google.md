# Alphabet/Google — Q4 2025 Earnings Summary

> **Q4 2025** (quarter ended December 31, 2025) | Reported: February 4, 2026

---

## Key Financials

| Metric | Actual | Estimate | Beat | YoY |
|--------|--------|----------|------|-----|
| Revenue | $113.83B | ~$111.1B | +2.4% | +18% |
| Diluted EPS | $2.82 | ~$2.62 | +7.6% | ~+30% |
| Net Income | $34.46B | — | — | +30% |
| Full-Year Revenue | $402.84B | — | — | First >$400B |

Q4 marked the first time Alphabet exceeded $400B in annual revenue. All headline metrics beat consensus, with EPS coming in roughly 8% above Street estimates. Revenue upside of ~$2.7B was led by Search acceleration and Cloud outperformance.

---

## Segment Performance

| Segment | Revenue | YoY | vs. Estimate |
|---------|---------|-----|--------------|
| Google Search | $63.10B | +17% | Beat (record) |
| YouTube Ads | $11.38B | +8.7% | In-line |
| Google Network | $7.83B | -1.5% | Miss |
| **Total Advertising** | **$82.28B** | **+13.5%** | Beat |
| Google Cloud | $17.70B | +48% | Beat |
| Other Bets | $370M | -7.5% | Miss |

**Google Search** posted a record quarter at $63.1B (+17% YoY), defying persistent concerns that generative AI competitors (OpenAI, Perplexity) would erode query volume. AI Overviews — integrated into Search results — showed no material monetization degradation; management noted ads alongside AI Overviews are performing in line with or above traditional search ads.

**YouTube Ads** decelerated to +8.7% from double-digit growth in prior quarters, reflecting the expected lap of strong 2024 U.S. election ad spending. Underlying demand appears healthy; Shorts monetization and connected-TV (CTV) inventory continue to ramp.

**Google Network** declined -1.5%, consistent with secular pressures on third-party ad inventory as programmatic demand shifts toward owned-and-operated surfaces.

**Google Cloud** at $17.7B (+48% YoY) was the standout, growing faster than AWS (+18%) and Azure (+~25%) in the quarter. Cloud backlog reached $240B, up 55% sequentially, implying accelerating enterprise commitment. The annual run rate exceeded $70B, firmly establishing Cloud as Alphabet's growth engine. Operating income from Cloud was not separately disclosed but margins have expanded materially as the segment scales.

**Other Bets** revenue fell -7.5% to $370M with an operating loss of -$3.61B — a sharp widening driven primarily by a $2.1B Waymo stock-based compensation charge. Operationally, Waymo reached 20M+ lifetime trips and 400K+ weekly paid rides, representing meaningful scale for the autonomous vehicle segment.

## Strategic Highlights

**Gemini AI Platform**
Gemini crossed **750M monthly active users** with 8M+ enterprise seats and 10B+ tokens processed per minute. Serving costs fell **78% over the course of 2025**, demonstrating rapid efficiency gains that are enabling broader deployment across Search, Cloud, and Workspace. CEO Sundar Pichai guided that Alphabet will be "supply constrained" in AI infrastructure throughout 2026 — consistent with the aggressive capex posture announced alongside results.

**Google Cloud AI**
Cloud AI momentum was the most concrete commercial AI signal of the quarter. Custom silicon accounts for approximately 58% of cloud AI compute, with TPU Ironwood (Alphabet's latest generation training/inference chip) in production. The Cloud backlog of $240B reflects multiyear enterprise AI commitments that de-risk near-term revenue visibility substantially.

**Capital Expenditure — The Market-Moving Disclosure**
Alphabet reported Q4 capex of $27.9B (FY 2025 total: $91.4B) and guided FY 2026 capex to **$175–185B** — roughly $55–65B above prior Street consensus of ~$119.5B. The magnitude of the revision shocked markets and was the primary driver of the post-earnings stock decline. Management framed the investment as demand-driven and necessary to serve a "supply constrained" AI market, but free cash flow compression was immediate: at $175B+ capex, FCF drops materially even if operating income continues expanding.

**Search Defense**
Despite competitive noise, Search revenue growth actually accelerated in Q4 (from +12% in Q3 to +17%). AI Overviews, launched in 2024 and scaled throughout 2025, appear to be increasing query depth and engagement rather than displacing ad inventory. Alphabet did not disclose AI Overviews query volume or ad attach rate separately.

**Waymo**
Waymo's 400K+ weekly rides represent a >10x increase from two years prior. The business remains pre-revenue at scale, with the $3.61B operating loss in Other Bets largely attributable to Waymo SBC. However, the ridership trajectory makes Waymo the most advanced commercial AV deployment globally.

## Market Reaction

**Earnings Day (February 4, 2026)**
- Stock entered earnings near all-time highs: ~**$343**
- Initial after-hours move: positive, on revenue and EPS beats
- Reversal: sharp decline as analysts processed the $175–185B capex guide
- **Following week close: ~$302–$313**, a decline of approximately **-7% to -10%**

The pattern mirrors Amazon's reaction to its own outsized capex guide reported the following day (February 5). In both cases, operational beats were overshadowed by FCF compression implied by AI infrastructure spending well above prior expectations.

**Analyst Consensus**
- **60 Buy / 7 Hold / 0 Sell** across 67 analysts covering the stock
- Median price target: ~**$380–$387**
- Highest targets: Goldman Sachs, RBC, Scotiabank at **$400**; JPMorgan at **$395**
- Bear case: capex trajectory compresses FCF for 2–3 years; ROI timeline uncertain
- Bull case: Cloud backlog of $240B provides multiyear revenue visibility; AI monetization yet to be fully priced

No major downgrades were issued post-earnings; the analyst community broadly maintained constructive ratings while acknowledging the near-term FCF headwind.

## Q1 2026 Directional Signals

Alphabet did not provide quantitative Q1 2026 guidance. Management commentary implied:

- Search growth will continue at elevated rates given AI Overviews tailwinds
- Cloud growth trajectory expected to sustain or accelerate given backlog conversion
- Capex will be front-loaded in 2026 (infrastructure build-out concentrated in H1)
- YouTube will anniversary election-cycle comparables beginning in Q2, enabling a reacceleration

*Note: Specific Q1 2026 revenue or EPS guidance was not disclosed in public filings.*

## Sector Implications

**Digital Advertising Market**
Search +17% and Total Ad Revenue +13.5% are among the strongest read-throughs for the digital advertising ecosystem in recent memory. The results suggest continued healthy demand from performance advertisers and brand budgets — a positive signal for Meta, Trade Desk, and other ad-tech players. YouTube's deceleration is election-cycle-specific and should not be read as structural; the underlying video advertising market remains robust.

**Cloud Competitive Positioning**
Google Cloud's +48% growth rate significantly exceeded AWS (+18%) and Azure (~+25%), marking Alphabet's strongest competitive performance in years. The $240B backlog (up 55% sequentially) signals that enterprise AI workload wins — many running on Gemini and TPU Ironwood infrastructure — are converting into long-duration commitments. This read-through is mixed for AWS and Azure: it validates AI cloud demand broadly but confirms Google Cloud is winning share.

**AI Infrastructure Demand**
The $175–185B capex guide is the most significant demand signal in the semiconductor and infrastructure supply chain this earnings cycle. At that scale, Alphabet is committing to sustained purchases of custom silicon (TPUs), Nvidia GPUs, networking equipment (InfiniBand/Ethernet switching), and data center real estate. Read-throughs are strongly positive for: TSMC (advanced packaging, CoWoS), Nvidia (H100/B200 successor demand), networking vendors (Arista, Broadcom), and data center REITs. The supply constraint language also suggests Alphabet would accelerate purchases if supply allowed — implying demand is not the binding constraint.

**Autonomous Vehicles**
Waymo's 400K+ weekly rides set a new commercial AV benchmark. The ridership scale puts pressure on competitors (Tesla FSD, Cruise re-launch, Mobileye) to demonstrate comparable real-world deployment. The $2.1B SBC charge within Other Bets suggests meaningful employee equity concentration in Waymo, aligning internal incentives with commercial success.
