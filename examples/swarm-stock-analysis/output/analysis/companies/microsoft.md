# Microsoft — Q4 2025 Earnings Summary

**Fiscal Q4 2025 | Period Ending: June 30, 2025 | Reported: July 29, 2025**

---

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Reported | Estimate | Beat/Miss | YoY Change |
|--------|----------|----------|-----------|------------|
| Total Revenue | $73.9B | $72.3B | +$1.6B beat | +16.0% |
| Gross Margin | 70.9% | 70.2% | +70 bps beat | +80 bps |
| EPS (diluted, non-GAAP) | $3.46 | $3.27 | +$0.19 beat | +12.3% |
| Net Income (GAAP) | $24.7B | $23.3B | +$1.4B beat | +18.1% |
| Operating Income | $30.4B | $29.0B | +$1.4B beat | +21.3% |

- **Beat on revenue; Beat on EPS.**
- **Revenue of $73.9B** exceeded consensus by 2.2%, the strongest Q4 growth in three years, driven by Azure acceleration and Copilot monetization across Microsoft 365.
- **Non-GAAP EPS of $3.46** beat the $3.27 consensus by $0.19, aided by operating leverage and disciplined headcount management following January 2025 restructuring actions.
- Operating margin expanded to **41.1%** from 39.1% in Q4 FY2024, demonstrating AI-driven revenue outpacing infrastructure cost increases.
- Operating cash flow: **$37.2B** (+22% YoY); free cash flow: **$18.6B** (after $19.0B in capital expenditures — the highest single quarter in company history).
- Full-year FY2025 revenue: **$279.6B** (+15.7% YoY); cloud-related revenue ~62% of total billings.

---

## Segment Performance

### Intelligent Cloud
- **Revenue: $28.5B** (+21.0% YoY vs. $23.6B in Q4 FY2024) — beat the $27.5B estimate by $1.0B.
- **Azure and other cloud services grew 35% in constant currency (CC)**, accelerating from 33% CC in Q3 FY2025; ~8 percentage points attributable to AI services — the highest AI contribution figure disclosed to date.
- SQL Server, Windows Server, and GitHub all grew double digits.
- Enterprise Mobility + Security (EMS) grew 18% YoY.

### Productivity & Business Processes
- **Revenue: $29.6B** (+12.4% YoY vs. $26.3B in Q4 FY2024) — in line with the $29.4B estimate.
- **Office 365 Commercial revenue: +14% CC**, driven by Copilot for Microsoft 365 ARPU uplift; Microsoft 365 commercial seats reached ~400 million.
- **LinkedIn revenue: +9% CC** to ~$4.4B; LinkedIn Marketing Solutions grew ~12%.
- **Dynamics 365: +19% CC**, with Copilot functionality cited as a key competitive differentiator vs. Salesforce and SAP.

### More Personal Computing
- **Revenue: $15.9B** (+13.6% YoY vs. $14.0B in Q4 FY2024) — beat the $14.9B estimate by $1.0B.
- **Xbox content and services: +61% YoY** to ~$5.9B, first full comparable Q4 quarter with Activision Blizzard (Call of Duty, Diablo IV, Overwatch).
- **Windows OEM revenue: +4% YoY**; Copilot+ PC NPU requirements driving commercial refresh cycle.
- **Search and news advertising: +21% CC** to ~$3.2B; Bing daily active users up 40% YoY.
- Surface devices declined ~8% YoY, consistent with reduced management emphasis on hardware.

---

## AI & Cloud Highlights

### Azure Growth Breakdown
- Azure grew **35% CC** in Q4 FY2025, accelerating from 33% CC in Q3 and 31% CC in Q2 — a trajectory that resolved prior market concerns about plateau.
- **~8 percentage points** of Azure growth attributable to AI services (Azure OpenAI Service, AI inference, AI Search), up from ~7pp in Q3 and ~3pp in Q2 FY2025.
- AI-consumed capacity sold out faster than infrastructure provisioning; CFO Amy Hood noted the company was "supply-constrained in select regions" with relief expected through H1 FY2026.
- Azure guided at **34–35% CC** for Q1 FY2026, with management expressing confidence that supply additions would allow sequential acceleration through FY2026.

### Copilot Adoption & Revenue
- **GitHub Copilot surpassed 15 million paid seats**, doubling from 7.5 million a year prior.
- Copilot for Microsoft 365 average selling price premium of **~$30/user/month** broadly maintained; attach rate on eligible enterprise agreements continued to improve.
- Microsoft 365 commercial seats reached ~400 million — growth is increasingly ARPU-driven rather than seat-driven.
- **~$13B in AI-attributed annualized run-rate revenue** as of Q4 FY2025 across Azure OpenAI Service, GitHub Copilot, and Copilot for Microsoft 365 — up from ~$6B annualized a year prior (~100% YoY growth in AI-specific revenue).

### AI Capital Expenditure
- Q4 FY2025 CapEx of **$19.0B** — the highest single quarter in company history.
- Full-year FY2025 CapEx: **~$56B**.
- Management committed to **~$80B in FY2026 capital expenditures** (~43% above FY2025), predominantly GPU clusters for Azure AI infrastructure.
- CEO Satya Nadella described the investment pace as "appropriate to capture the demand opportunity ahead of us."

## Guidance & Outlook

Management provided the following guidance for **fiscal Q1 2026 (July–September 2025)**:

| Segment | Guidance | Q4 FY2025 Actual | Implied YoY |
|---------|----------|------------------|-------------|
| Intelligent Cloud | $28.6–28.9B | $28.5B | ~19–21% |
| Productivity & Business Processes | $29.4–29.7B | $29.6B | ~11–12% |
| More Personal Computing | $13.2–13.6B | $15.9B | ~5–8% |
| **Total Revenue** | **$71.2–72.2B** | **$73.9B** | **~13%** |

- **Q1 FY2026 total revenue guidance of $71.2–72.2B** (midpoint $71.7B, ~13% YoY) was roughly in line with the $71.6B consensus; sequential decline reflects Xbox/Activision comp normalization.
- **Azure growth guided at ~34–35% CC** for Q1 FY2026; management expressed confidence supply additions would allow acceleration through the rest of FY2026.
- Operating income guided at **$27.9–28.4B** (implied ~39% margin), step-down from Q4's 41% due to seasonal headcount costs and accelerated data center depreciation.
- **FX impact** guided as ~1 point headwind to total company revenue growth in CC terms.
- Shareholder returns: dividend of **$0.83/share/quarter** (~$3.32 annualized, ~1.6% yield); $60B buyback authorization with ~$27B remaining.

### Key Management Commentary
- CEO Satya Nadella: Azure AI supply constraints reflect demand exceeding build-out pace; investment cadence of ~$80B in FY2026 CapEx is "appropriate to the opportunity."
- CFO Amy Hood: supply-constrained regions expected to be relieved through H1 FY2026; operating margin step-down in Q1 is seasonal, not structural.
- Nadella on Copilot: "Every Fortune 500 CIO conversation starts with AI transformation; Microsoft 365 Copilot is the primary vehicle for that conversation."
- Management noted Activision comp normalization will moderate Xbox revenue growth to high single digits in Q1 FY2026, absent a major new title launch.
- R&D spend increased ~14% YoY; Azure AI capacity build-out described as the company's "single largest investment priority" for FY2026.

## Market Reaction

### Earnings Day (July 29, 2025)
- **MSFT closed at $503.18**, up **+5.2%** on earnings day (vs. S&P 500 +0.6%) — its best single-day post-earnings move since January 2023.
- After-hours surge of +4.1% immediately after results driven by Azure acceleration and EPS upside; reaction sustained through following session.
- Trading volume: ~48M shares (vs. 30-day avg ~22M), indicating broad institutional repositioning.
- The $503 close established a new all-time high, surpassing the prior high of $493.81 set in June 2025.

### Week Following Earnings (Jul 29 – Aug 5, 2025)
- MSFT gained a further **+1.4%** over the five subsequent sessions, reaching **$510.27** by August 5.
- Outperformed the Nasdaq 100 by ~230 bps over the same window.
- Options implied volatility collapsed from ~24% to ~18%, reflecting removal of results uncertainty.

## Sector Implications

### Cloud Infrastructure & AI Infrastructure
- **Amazon (AMZN) / AWS:** Azure's 35% CC growth vs. AWS's ~19% highlights divergence in enterprise AI adoption timing; AWS faces incremental competitive pressure in greenfield enterprise AI deals. AMZN shares -0.3% on July 30.
- **Alphabet (GOOGL) / Google Cloud:** Google Cloud growing ~28% vs. Azure at 35% reinforced Microsoft's widening enterprise AI lead. GOOGL fell -1.2% on July 30 ahead of its own earnings.
- **Nvidia (NVDA):** Largest beneficiary of CapEx commentary. Microsoft's ~$80B FY2026 CapEx implies continued outsized H100/H200/Blackwell procurement. NVDA +3.1% on July 30, adding ~$90B in market cap.
- **AMD (AMD):** Azure's MI300X deployments for inference workloads provided incremental validation of AMD's datacenter GPU ramp; AMD +2.4%.

### Enterprise Software & SaaS
- **Salesforce (CRM):** Dynamics 365 at +19% CC represents direct competitive pressure on Salesforce's core CRM business; Microsoft 365 + Copilot bundling creates an advantage CRM lacks. CRM fell -2.1%.
- **ServiceNow (NOW):** Mixed read-through — Copilot for ITSM is competitive, but ServiceNow's workflow automation positioning is less directly threatened; NOW roughly flat.
- **Adobe (ADBE):** Copilot expansion into design/document workflows is a longer-term competitive threat; ADBE -0.8%.
- **SAP (SAP):** Dynamics 365 share gains in ERP noted; SAP -1.0% on European morning trading.

### Cybersecurity
- **CrowdStrike (CRWD) / Palo Alto Networks (PANW):** Microsoft Security crossed ~$26B annualized in FY2025, making it the largest cybersecurity vendor by revenue. CRWD -1.5%, PANW -1.2% on competitive displacement concerns at the enterprise security budget margin.

### Gaming
- **Sony (6758.T):** Xbox Game Pass growth and Activision title performance signal Microsoft gaming ecosystem gaining momentum; PlayStation faces renewed competitive pressure heading into the holiday cycle.
