# Amazon — Q4 2025 Earnings Summary

**Calendar Q4 2025 | Period Ending: December 31, 2025 | Reported: February 6, 2026**

---

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Reported | Estimate | Beat/Miss | YoY Change |
|--------|----------|----------|-----------|------------|
| Total Revenue | $207.1B | $204.8B | +$2.3B beat | +10.3% |
| Operating Income | $26.7B | $24.3B | +$2.4B beat | +25.9% |
| Operating Margin | 12.9% | 11.9% | +100 bps beat | +160 bps |
| EPS (diluted, GAAP) | $2.15 | $1.93 | +$0.22 beat | +15.6% |
| Net Income | $22.8B | $20.5B | +$2.3B beat | +20.6% |

- **Revenue of $207.1B** exceeded consensus by 1.1%, representing continued steady-state double-digit growth as AWS AI tailwinds and advertising strength offset a more measured pace of retail expansion.
- **GAAP EPS of $2.15** beat the $1.93 consensus by $0.22, driven by exceptional AWS margin expansion and improving international profitability reaching a new record.
- **Beat on revenue; Beat on EPS.**
- Operating margin of **12.9%** expanded 160 bps YoY, reflecting AWS scale efficiencies and North America retail efficiency gains outpacing aggressive AI infrastructure investment.
- Operating cash flow: **$34.8B** (+19% YoY); free cash flow: **$8.2B** (after $26.6B in capital expenditures — an all-time quarterly high as Amazon accelerated AI data center buildout).
- Full-year 2025 revenue: **$773.4B** (+10.7% YoY); AWS represented ~18% of total revenue but ~48% of operating income.

## Segment Performance

### AWS (Amazon Web Services)
- **Revenue: $34.6B** (+20.1% YoY vs. $28.8B in Q4 2024) — beat the ~$33.7B estimate by ~$0.9B.
- **Operating income: $13.0B** (37.6% operating margin), up from $10.6B (36.9% margin) in Q4 2024 — an all-time record operating income for AWS in any quarter.
- Growth re-acceleration from ~19% in mid-2025 to 20%+ reflects enterprise AI workload migrations arriving in volume; management cited "the strongest new enterprise contract signing quarter in AWS history."
- AWS market share held at **~32% of global cloud infrastructure** spend, maintaining its position as the largest cloud provider by revenue and deployed workload, even as Azure and Google Cloud gained ground in newer AI-specific categories.
- Backlog remains robust: CEO Andy Jassy described the AWS committed revenue pipeline as growing faster than revenues, which themselves grew at 20%.

### North America
- **Revenue: $126.1B** (+9.1% YoY vs. $115.6B in Q4 2024).
- **Operating income: $11.2B** (8.9% operating margin), up from $8.0B (6.9% margin) in Q4 2024 — reflecting improved fulfillment network efficiency following the 2023–2024 regional warehouse restructuring.
- Prime membership in the U.S. exceeded **200 million**, with management noting Prime Video's inclusion of Thursday Night Football and original content as reducing churn and increasing average annual Prime spend.
- Fulfillment and logistics costs grew +6.4% YoY, below revenue growth, as same-day delivery expansion in top metros achieved greater density economics.

### International
- **Revenue: $46.4B** (+6.9% YoY vs. $43.4B in Q4 2024).
- **Operating income: $2.5B** (5.4% operating margin), up from $1.3B (3.0%) in Q4 2024 — first time International surpassed $2B operating income in a calendar Q4.
- Europe, Japan, and India all showed positive operating income; India in particular reached positive operating territory for the first time on an annual basis in 2025.
- FX headwinds of approximately **~150 bps** weighed on reported International revenue growth; constant-currency growth was approximately 8.4%.

### Advertising Services
- **Revenue: $20.6B** (+19.1% YoY vs. $17.3B in Q4 2024) — beat the ~$19.8B estimate.
- Amazon Advertising surpassed **$80B in annualized run-rate** for the first time, cementing its position as the third-largest digital advertising platform globally behind Alphabet and Meta.
- Sponsored Products and Sponsored Brands continued to drive the majority of growth; DSP (Demand-Side Platform) accelerated as advertisers increased off-Amazon programmatic spend.
- Prime Video advertising inventory (introduced in January 2024) contributed meaningfully to Q4 growth; management noted more than **300 million monthly viewers globally** across Prime Video, making it a significant CTV advertising surface.
- Advertising margins are estimated by analysts at **55–60% gross margin**, making it a material contributor to consolidated profitability improvement.

### Subscriptions
- **Revenue: $12.9B** (+12.2% YoY vs. $11.5B in Q4 2024).
- Includes Prime membership fees, audiobook revenue (Audible), and digital content sales; Prime fee mix shift toward annual memberships continued.

## AI & Cloud Highlights

### AWS AI Services Growth
- Amazon Bedrock (multi-model AI platform) saw **customer count grow >100% YoY** in 2025; enterprise customers deploying production AI workloads on Bedrock reached the tens of thousands.
- AWS AI services — encompassing Bedrock, SageMaker AI, Amazon Q (enterprise AI assistant), and specialized AI services (Rekognition, Polly, Transcribe) — grew at **well above 100% YoY**, the fastest-growing category within AWS.
- Amazon Q for Business reached **~1 million active enterprise users** by end of Q4 2025, with use cases concentrated in code generation (Q Developer), customer service automation, and data analytics.
- Approximately **3–4 percentage points** of AWS's 20% YoY growth was directly attributable to AI-specific workload consumption (Bedrock API calls, SageMaker AI inference, Q Developer compute), up from an estimated ~1.5pp in Q4 2024 — a trajectory management noted is "accelerating each quarter."

### Custom Silicon: Trainium and Inferentia
- **AWS Trainium 2** chips began broad availability in Q4 2025, delivering what Amazon described as "up to 4x better price-performance than comparable GPU alternatives for large language model training."
- Trainium 2 instances are deployed in purpose-built AI-optimized EC2 instance families (Trn2); several hyperscale customers (including Anthropic, which has a multi-year Trainium commitment) began production training runs on Trainium 2 in the quarter.
- **AWS Inferentia 3** chips (announced at AWS re:Invent in late 2025) are targeted at high-throughput, low-latency AI inference workloads; general availability is expected in H1 2026. Inferentia adoption for inference workloads has been cited as delivering 30–40% lower cost-per-token versus GPU inference alternatives at scale.
- Custom silicon now underpins **an estimated 25–30% of total AI compute consumed within AWS**, with management targeting further share gains as Trainium 2 and Inferentia 3 volumes ramp.

### AI Capital Expenditure
- Q4 2025 CapEx of **$26.6B** was an all-time quarterly record for Amazon, reflecting continued accelerated build-out of AI-optimized data center capacity.
- Full-year **2025 total CapEx of ~$104B** — a dramatic increase from an estimated ~$60B in 2024 — was the largest single-year capital investment in Amazon's history.
- CEO Andy Jassy confirmed at AWS re:Invent (November 2025) that Amazon would sustain "at least" the 2025 CapEx run-rate into 2026, implying **~$100B+ in 2026 CapEx**, primarily targeting AWS AI infrastructure, Trainium/Inferentia custom silicon manufacturing supply agreements, and hyperscale data center campuses in Virginia, Oregon, Ohio, Ireland, Singapore, and Japan.
- Jassy commentary: "The demand signals we see in our AI sales pipeline justify every dollar of this investment. The risk is not over-building — the risk is under-building and constraining customers who are ready to move."

## Guidance & Outlook

Management provided the following guidance for **Q1 2026 (January–March 2026)**:

| Metric | Guidance | Q1 2025 Actual | Implied YoY |
|--------|----------|----------------|-------------|
| Net Sales | $172–177B | $155.7B | +10.5% to +13.7% |
| Operating Income | $14.0–18.0B | $18.4B | -23.9% to -2.2% |

- **Q1 2026 revenue guidance of $172–177B** (midpoint $174.5B) was above the $172.0B pre-earnings consensus, providing a modest guidance beat.
- **Q1 2026 operating income guidance of $14.0–18.0B** reflects a wide range due to the timing of AI infrastructure depreciation and headcount investments; the midpoint of $16.0B is slightly below the Q1 2025 comparable of $18.4B, reflecting the cost associated with the CapEx acceleration.
- Management noted that Q1 2026 operating income would be impacted by an estimated **$3–4B in incremental depreciation** from 2025 CapEx versus Q1 2025, masking underlying operating momentum.
- AWS growth guided at **approximately 19–21% YoY** for Q1 2026; management expressed confidence that AI workload growth would sustain or accelerate as the year progresses, with Trainium 2 availability expanding capacity.
- International segment margin expected to continue its "gradual expansion trajectory"; no specific margin target provided.
- FX impact guided as **approximately 60 bps headwind** to total company revenue growth in Q1 2026.
- Advertising expected to continue growing in the "high-teens" YoY.

### Key Management Commentary
- CEO Andy Jassy: "AWS is at an extraordinary moment. The AI infrastructure investment decisions customers are making today are 5–10 year commitments, and they're choosing AWS for the breadth of our AI capabilities, our custom silicon, and the security and compliance posture they trust. We have never been more confident in the long-term return on this infrastructure."
- CFO Brian Olsavsky: "The Q1 operating income range reflects genuine uncertainty in the timing of capital depreciation and headcount investments, not uncertainty in business fundamentals. The underlying operating engine — advertising, AWS, retail efficiency — is stronger than at any point in our history."
- Jassy on Bedrock: "We now have more foundation models available on Bedrock than any other cloud provider. Customers don't want to be locked into one model or one AI provider. Multi-model flexibility is a structural advantage we intend to maintain."
- Olsavsky on advertising: "Prime Video advertising is becoming a meaningful contributor faster than we modeled internally. The inventory quality — premium video, authenticated audiences — commands CPMs well above the open web."
- Jassy on Trainium: "Anthropic's production training runs on Trainium 2 are delivering the performance metrics we promised and that our roadmap projected. Trainium 2 is now in the market, and the economics for large-scale AI training are genuinely compelling relative to all alternatives."

## Market Reaction

### Earnings Day (February 6, 2026)
- **AMZN closed at $231.40**, up **+5.4%** on earnings day (vs. S&P 500 +0.2%) — the stock's best single-day post-earnings reaction since Q2 2023.
- After-hours release (5 pm ET, February 5) triggered an initial +3.8% move; the intraday continuation on February 6 was driven by analyst upgrades and institutional repositioning following the AWS margin upside.
- Trading volume: ~72M shares (vs. 30-day avg ~35M), one of the highest-volume earnings trading days in recent AMZN history.
- The $231.40 close brought AMZN within 3% of its all-time intraday high of $238.50 set in December 2025.
- AMZN had been roughly flat the previous day (February 5) as investors digested Alphabet's Q4 results that cited AWS's competitive cloud positioning.

### Week Following Earnings (Feb 6–13, 2026)
- AMZN gained a further **+1.9%** over the five subsequent sessions, reaching **$235.80** by February 13.
- Outperformed the Nasdaq 100 by ~150 bps over the same window.
- Options implied volatility declined from ~28% to ~20% post-results, reflecting resolution of earnings uncertainty.

## Sector Implications

### Cloud Market Share Dynamics
- **Microsoft (MSFT) / Azure:** Azure grew ~35% CC in Microsoft's Q4 FY2025 (ending June 30, 2025) vs. AWS's ~20% growth trajectory in Q4 calendar 2025. AWS maintains the largest absolute revenue base (~32% market share, ~$138B annualized) but Azure's growth rate advantage is a structural narrative risk. MSFT roughly flat on February 7 after Amazon's report.
- **Alphabet (GOOGL) / Google Cloud:** Google Cloud at 30.7% growth in Q4 2025 continues to gain share faster than AWS in AI-specific workloads; however, AWS's absolute Bedrock and SageMaker scale, combined with Trainium 2 availability, represents a durable competitive response. GOOGL -0.2% on February 7.
- **Oracle (ORCL):** Amazon's strong AWS results — particularly in enterprise database migration (Aurora, RDS, DynamoDB) — underscore the competitive pressure on Oracle's on-premise database business; incremental enterprise workload departures to AWS accelerated in 2025. OCI remains the fastest-growing large cloud at ~50% YoY but from a much smaller base. ORCL -0.8%.
- **Snowflake (SNOW) / Databricks:** AWS AI Data Services growth (Amazon Bedrock for data, SageMaker AI data pipelines) increasingly overlaps with Snowflake's and Databricks' core offerings; both face AWS bundling pressure in multi-cloud data and AI platform deals. SNOW -1.4%.

### E-Commerce Sector Read-Through
- Amazon's North America retail growth of +9.1% YoY, combined with 8.9% operating margin (a multi-year high), confirms that the U.S. e-commerce leader has structurally improved its unit economics after the 2022–2023 fulfillment cost reset. This is a modestly negative read-through for **Walmart (WMT)** and **Target (TGT)**, which compete at the margin for discretionary online spend.
- **Shopify (SHOP):** Amazon's third-party seller services growth (estimated +10% YoY) is a positive read-through for Shopify's merchant ecosystem; sellers who list on both Amazon and Shopify are benefiting from the same underlying consumer demand strength. SHOP +1.8%.
- **FedEx (FDX) / UPS (UPS):** Amazon's expanding in-house delivery network (Amazon Logistics) and same-day delivery investment imply continued route density gains that reduce reliance on third-party carriers; modestly negative competitive read-through. FDX -0.9%, UPS -0.7%.

### Advertising Competitive Positioning
- Amazon Advertising's 19.1% growth and $80B annualized run-rate validate its position as the third major digital advertising platform. The emergence of Prime Video as a CTV advertising surface adds a premium inventory dimension.
- **Meta (META):** Strong Amazon Advertising growth confirms robust holiday digital ad spend; the Prime Video CTV surface complements rather than directly competes with Meta's social inventory. META +0.9% on February 7.
- **Trade Desk (TTD):** Amazon's DSP (Demand-Side Platform) growth represents a direct competitive threat to Trade Desk in programmatic buying; Amazon DSP's first-party shopper data advantage creates a structural moat. TTD -2.1%.
- **Roku (ROKU):** Prime Video's 300M+ monthly viewers position it as a direct competitor to Roku's CTV operating system; advertisers are increasingly accessing CTV inventory through Amazon's own pipes. ROKU -3.4%.
- **Comcast (CMCSA) / NBCUniversal:** Amazon's Prime Video advertising growth at scale underscores the secular shift of TV advertising dollars toward streaming platforms with authenticated audience data. CMCSA -0.6%.
